Latest: Why Fluor Corporation Could Double by 2027

April 02, 2025

Fluor Corporation (FLR), a $6B mid-cap industrials player, sits at an inflection point. Its $19B project backlog—up 10% year-over-year—signals robust demand in infrastructure and energy. Q1 2025 earnings showed a 15% revenue bump to $3.8B, yet the stock trades at a P/E of 12, below the sector average of 18. Retail investors might see a growth story; hedge funds could spot undervaluation. Risks? Margin pressure from labor costs. Still, if Fluor executes, $12B in new awards could push shares from $40 to $80 by 2027.
